Key Findings

  • 📉 Revenue growth has stagnated since 2021, with total revenue peaking in 2023 before declining in 2024, indicating limited top-line expansion in Harley-Davidson’s core markets.
  • 📊 Operating margins remain structurally strong but are deteriorating, falling from ~19% in 2022 to 12.53% in 2024, signalling increasing cost pressure and weakening pricing leverage.

Data-Driven Recommendations

  • 🚀 Targeted premium growth investment: Allocate approximately USD 450–550M annually toward expanding premium mid-weight motorcycle (600cc - 1000cc) offerings and selective international markets, aiming to restore sustainable revenue growth without diluting brand positioning.
  • 🧩 Margin-accretive ecosystem expansion: Invest USD 250–300M in Parts & Accessories and digital customer engagement platforms to reinforce high-margin, recurring revenue streams with limited capital intensity.
  • ⚖️ Balance-sheet risk reduction: Direct USD 500–650M of operating cash flow toward gradual deleveraging, with the objective of reducing financial risk and improving resilience in a high-interest-rate environment.

Business Context & Story

1. Who is Harley-Davidson today?

Harley-Davidson is a premium motorcycle manufacturer with a strong global brand and a loyal customer base. The company focuses on heavyweight motorcycles, supported by parts, accessories, and financial services. Its strategy prioritises brand value, premium pricing, and profitability rather than mass-market volume.

2. What is happening in the market?

Between 2021 and 2024, the motorcycle industry has faced a more challenging environment:

  • Demand has normalised after the post-COVID surge.
  • Higher interest rates have reduced discretionary consumer spending.
  • Competition from global players such as BMW and Yamaha has intensified.
  • For premium manufacturers, these conditions limit revenue growth and put pressure on pricing power and cost structures.

3. Why BMW and Yamaha?

  • Relevant peer benchmarks: Both BMW Motorrad and Yamaha are global motorcycle manufacturers with strong brand equity and comparable exposure to discretionary consumer demand.
  • Contrasting growth profiles: BMW and Yamaha have demonstrated stronger post-2021 revenue momentum, providing a useful contrast to Harley-Davidson’s revenue stagnation.
  • Industry vs. company diagnosis: Comparing Harley-Davidson against these peers helps distinguish whether performance trends reflect broader industry conditions or company-specific strategic limitations.
  • Strategic insight generation: The comparison enables evaluation of trade-offs between premium positioning, product diversification, and growth execution across leading players.

4. The core business challenge

Harley-Davidson currently faces a clear strategic trade-off:

  • On one hand, the company has maintained strong profitability and cash generation.
  • On the other hand, revenue growth has stalled, and recent margin pressure raises concerns about sustainability.

5. Why this analysis matters

This analysis matters because Harley-Davidson’s future performance depends on how well it can:

  • Defend profitability while costs and interest rates remain high.
  • Reignite growth without diluting its premium brand positioning.
  • Maintain financial flexibility given its relatively high leverage.
By analysing recent financial performance and market signals, this dashboard aims to identify where the strategy is working, where risks are emerging, and what adjustments may be required going forward.

Data Acquisition, Governance & Technical Skills

1. Data Sources

This project uses publicly available financial and market data to analyse Harley-Davidson’s recent performance. Primary data sources include:

  • Yahoo Finance: Annual Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow data (2021–2024), derived from audited SEC filings.
  • Yahoo Finance market data: Historical stock price data for Harley-Davidson and selected competitors.
These sources were chosen due to their reliability, transparency, and relevance for financial performance analysis.

2. Data Scope & Timeframe

The analysis focuses on the 2021–2024 period, capturing a critical post-pandemic environment characterised by:

  • Normalisation of consumer demand.
  • Rising interest rates.
  • Increased pressure on margins and discretionary spending.
Although this limits long-term historical comparison, the timeframe provides highly relevant insights into Harley-Davidson’s current financial health and strategic positioning.

3. Data Preparation & Transformation

All data preparation was performed using Python (pandas, numpy) to ensure a fully reproducible, code-based workflow. The data processing followed a structured sequence:

  • Data acquisition and validation: Financial data were sourced from publicly available, audited SEC filings via Yahoo Finance and cross-checked for consistency.
  • Data standardisation: All financial figures were converted to USD (billions) to ensure comparability across companies and reporting periods.
  • Temporal alignment: Fiscal year data were aligned across Harley-Davidson, BMW, and Yamaha to ensure consistent year-over-year comparison.
  • Data quality checks: Missing values were assessed and confirmed absent in core financial metrics; outliers (e.g. post-COVID rebound effects) were reviewed to ensure economic plausibility rather than automatically removed.
  • Final dataset preparation: Cleaned and validated datasets were finalised for analysis and visualisation.
After processing, the prepared datasets were exported from Python and embedded directly into JavaScript variables within the dashboard’s single self-contained HTML file. This approach ensures data integrity, portability, and full offline functionality, in line with the project’s technical requirements.

4. Analytical Approach

The analysis follows a decision-focused analytical framework:

  • Descriptive analytics to summarise recent trends in revenue, profitability, cash flow, and leverage.
  • Diagnostic analytics to explain performance changes through comparative analysis with key competitors, identifying drivers of margin pressure and revenue stagnation.
  • Predictive analytics to assess potential future outcomes using a simple supervised regression model, providing a directional outlook on operating margin trends rather than precise forecasts.
  • Prescriptive analytics to translate analytical findings into actionable strategic recommendations, highlighting trade-offs and risk considerations for management.
This structured methodology ensures that insights move beyond reporting, directly supporting executive-level decision-making.

5. Tools & Technical Implementation

The technical workflow combines data analytics and web-based BI development:

  • Python (pandas, numpy) for data cleaning, transformation, metric calculation, building ML predictive model and export embedded JS data.
  • Plotly for creating interactive and static visualisations.
  • HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for dashboard structure, styling, and interactivity.
To meet the project’s submission requirements, all CSS and JavaScript were embedded directly within a single self-contained HTML file, with datasets stored as in-file JavaScript objects. This ensures the dashboard:
  • Runs offline.
  • Has no external data dependencies.
  • Meets file size and deployment constraints.

6. Data Governance & Ethical Considerations

  • All datasets are aggregated, non-personal, and publicly available, ensuring full GDPR compliance.
  • No personal, sensitive, or proprietary data was collected or processed.
  • Data sources are clearly attributed and used strictly for academic analysis.
  • Ethical considerations were addressed by avoiding data manipulation, misrepresentation, or speculative inference beyond empirical evidence.

7. Limitations

  • The analysis is limited to public financial and market data, which may not capture internal operational efficiencies, customer sentiment, or product-level profitability.
  • Reported financial data may include one-off or non-recurring items.
  • Short historical horizon limits long-term trend extrapolation.
These limitations are acknowledged when interpreting results and form the basis for proposed future extensions of the analysis.

Insights & Strategic Recommendations

This section synthesises key financial and market diagnostics to provide prescriptive, data-driven strategic recommendations for Harley-Davidson’s long-term competitiveness.

Key Insight 1: Liquidity Constraint Limits Strategic Flexibility

BMW maintains significantly higher cash reserves (approximately USD 19.3bn) compared to Harley-Davidson (approximately USD 1.6bn), providing BMW with greater flexibility to fund capital-intensive initiatives such as electrification, R&D, and acquisitions. Despite carrying higher total debt, BMW’s liquidity position enables more aggressive long-term investment.

Strategic Recommendation 1: Given this liquidity gap, Harley-Davidson should adopt a highly selective, capital-efficient growth strategy, prioritising asset-light manufacturing partnerships and outsourcing non-core activities to preserve cash while maintaining strategic optionality.

Key Insight 2: Profitability Strength Is Not Translating into Growth

While Harley-Davidson demonstrates relatively strong operating margins compared to some competitors, this profitability advantage has not translated into sustained revenue growth or improved market valuation. This suggests a disconnect between internal cost discipline and external growth perception.

Strategic Recommendation 2: Harley-Davidson should reallocate a portion of its margin advantage toward targeted growth initiatives, including product innovation and international market expansion, ensuring profitability is actively leveraged to drive top-line momentum rather than solely preserved.

Key Insight 3: Valuation Discount Reflects Market Growth Expectations

While Harley-Davidson demonstrates relatively strong operating margins compared to some competitors, this profitability advantage has not translated into sustained revenue growth or improved market valuation.
